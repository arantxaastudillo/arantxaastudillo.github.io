Sorting and Arranging

On March 15, our class split into groups of four for a new class activity. Everyone in the group brought around ten different small items; two group members brought something edible, and the other to brought something non-edible. My group sorted our items in seven different ways according to some criteria/quality.

First, we piled all of out items together with no order, this is the 'data' pile. All data is here, however it is not sorted.

Next, we specified that all the 'data' can be split into two by one quality; is it edible or not?

We figured that the relationships of the 'data' might be more clear if we sorted it according to the type. Is the 'data' a gummy bear, a penny, a paper clip, hard candy or a sunflower seed?

Next, we sorted the 'data' in a way that might be a bit more legible; by color.

Next, we sorted the 'data' from most durable (at the top), to least durable (at the bottom).

Here, we arranged the 'data' in a more visually legible way; by color and type.

Lastly, we arranged the 'data' so that it is asthetically pleasing.
