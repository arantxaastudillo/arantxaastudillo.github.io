Reading 2

1. Given the Wikipedia definitions on data and informations, what is your own take on this and specify what is the difference between data and information.
According to Wikipedia, data means “ a set of values of qualitative or quantitative variables”. Information, according to Wikipedia, means “any entity or form that resolves uncertainty or provides the answer to a question of some kind”. I think the difference between the two is that data can be broken down into parts while information is the understanding of a collected data.

2.From the Manovich reading, what is the difference between visualization and mapping?
Manovich describes visualization as something that is visually represented while mapping is the documentation of data. The difference between the two is that visualization (noun) is when something is presented visually and mapping (verb) is the process of organizing the data.

3.When Manovich states that visualization is the anti-sublime art, what is he referring to? Do you agree with this notion?
When Manovich mentions that visualization is the “anti-sublime art” he means to take something that is mean to be represented as unreal and represented as something that is understandable and real.

4.When Manovich remarks on the ‘dark-side’ of mapping, what is he referring to?
To Manovich, the “dark-side” of mapping is the ability to map chaos without really understanding what its composed of.

Laurie Frick
Frick was an engineer who became an artist later in life. She created a device that is attached to a person’s head to monitor their sleep patterns. By doing this, she used (B) Data as Process and Exploration and she also (E) experimented in self-surveillance. Zeo, the company that makes Fitbits, admired her work and contacted her and asked if she would like help to analyse and process the data. By agreeing to this, she was able to further perfect her work by (A) Crowd Sourcing with Zeo. She then took the data that she gathered and organized it into a visual map that she calls art. Laurie’s ability to take scientific data and turn it into something interactive and applicable is something to take seriously because it could help millions of people change the way they monitor their sleep habits.

Feltron
He gathered lots of data on his own life and experience. Using scanned images of documents he saved, he was able to plot graphs of data using the documents and images of dates and important life events. Using the graphs he was able to come up with an algorithm that generated images that he calls art. These images are very abstract in form and don’t really follow any kind of naturally occurring organic shapes. He is good at creating data narratives. He creates a map to record the his father’s life experience in his life spans by collecting the data from passports, calendars, photos, leftover documents, address, receipts, EKG report, etc. D.
